NILAGANG BABOY

Nilagang Baboy or Pork Nilaga is a classic Filipino soup wherein pork (any cut) is boiled until tender and vegetables such as but not limited to potatoes, cabbage or pechay, beans, and green onions are added for flavors. Usually served with steamed rice for a satisfying lunch or dinner. This dish is among the many comfort foods popular throughout the country, along with Tinola, Sinigang, and Bulalo.

Nilagang Baboy Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork ribs
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 large yellow onion sliced thickly
  • 1 teaspoon peppercorn
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 3 pcs medium potatoes
  • 2 pcs green saba bananas
  • 6 pieces Baguio beans / green beans
  • 1 stalk onion leek or green onion
  • 1-2 bunch pechay or cabbage
  • salt and pepper to taste

How to Make Nilagang Baboy Step-By-Step

  1. Place pork ribs in a large pot. Add just enough water to submerge. Bring to a boil over high heat.
  2. Once the scum has come out, turn off the heat, throw away the water, and give the pork ribs and pot a quick rinse.
  3. Return the pork ribs to the pot and add about 8 cups of water.
  4. Add the onion, peppercorn, and fish sauce. Bring to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, cover and turn the heat to low. Let it simmer until meat is fork tender.
  5. Meanwhile, prepare the vegetables and bananas. Wash and peel the potatoes and then cut them into quarters.
  6. Cut off the ends of the bananas and wash thoroughly, then cut each into 3 pieces diagonally, unpeeled. Soak both potatoes and saba bananas in water until ready to use.
  7. Cut off the stem of the pechay and wash the leaves thoroughly. Remove the ends of the beans and cut into about 3-inch lengths. Remove the root part of the onion leek and cut it into 2-inch pieces.
  8. Add the green bananas and cook for 5 minutes, then add the potatoes and cook for another 5 minutes and then add the green beans and cook for another 5 minutes or until the potatoes are cooked.
  9. Season with salt and pepper as needed then lastly add the green onions and pechay. Cover the pot and let simmer for a minute before turning it off.
  10. Serve in a large soup pot or individual bowls. Enjoy with rice and a spicy fish sauce dip.
